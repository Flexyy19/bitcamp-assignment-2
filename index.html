My Favorite Book

  1. Interview with the Devil

    Neil Gaiman

    presents a provocative and engaging dialogue between a journalist and the Devil. Through their conversation, the book explores themes of morality, human nature, and existential questions.

  2. The Four Agreements

    Don Miguel Ruiz

    This book is a guide to personal development and self-improvement, offering practical advice based on ancient wisdom. It provides simple yet profound principles to help readers achieve greater happiness and freedom in their lives.

  3. Talent Is Overrated

    Geoff Colvin

    This book explores how practice and hard work, rather than innate talent, are the key factors in achieving high levels of success. The book argues that deliberate practice and perseverance are crucial for excellence in any field.

  4. The Brothers Karamazov

    Fyodor Dostoevsky

    This book is a profound novel that explores themes of faith, doubt, and morality through the lives of the Karamazov family. The story revolves around the complex relationships between the father and his three sons, delving into philosophical and existential questions that challenge the characters' beliefs and values.

  5. Crime and Punishment

    Fyodor Dostoevsky

    This book is a novel that follows the psychological torment of Raskolnikov, a young man who commits a murder and grapples with guilt and redemption. The story delves into themes of morality, justice, and the human condition, examining the impact of crime on both the perpetrator and society.
